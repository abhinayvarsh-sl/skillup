Core Constraints & Guidelines
  • Low Risk Approach: Minimal changes to paid program pages. Focus on SkillUp/FRS funnels.
  • SEO Preservation: SkillUp URLs and "Free" messaging must remain intact on entry pages.
  • Design System: Reuse existing SLH library patterns. Only design new states and marketing surfaces.
  • Pricing Model: Strict Flat Ladder (Lite → Pro → Ultimate). No category packs.

✅ In Scope

  • Funnel Optimization: SkillUp → Evolve plan page.
  • FRS Integration: Contextual banners in articles.
  • LMS Nudges: Dashboard locked states, upgrade modals.
  • B2B: Enterprise entry point & "Contact Sales" flow.

❌ Out of Scope

  • Redesigning Core Program Pages: Keep existing layout.
  • Self-Serve Enterprise Checkout: Lead capture only.
  • Complex Pricing: No hierarchical packs or bundles.

Product Snapshot

Primary Goal PLG

B2B Lead Generation

Identify Product Qualified Leads (PQL) via work-email capture.

  • Mechanism: Unlock advanced workshops in exchange for verified work email.
  • Success: Seamless routing to sales teams.
UX Requirements
  • Prompt must be value-driven ("Unlock feature"), not intrusive.
  • Clear visual feedback upon verification (Toast + Unlocked Badge).
Secondary Goal B2C Revenue

Subscription Conversion

Convert free users to paid subscribers.

  • Flow: Freemium (Lite) → Pro Trial → Paid Pro.
  • Strategy: "Pro" is the hero plan. Ultimate is a secondary upsell.
UX Requirements
  • Highlight Pro plan visually.
  • Use contextual nudges rather than hard paywalls where possible.
Scale

High-Volume Nudging

Hundreds of thousands of monthly users.

  • Design prompts to be lightweight, repeatable, and non-annoying.
  • Events must be accurate to avoid user frustration.
Reality Check

SkillUp UX & IA Implications

  • Current SkillUp has overlap between Homepage and "Explore".
  • Design Decision: Lean toward a cleaner "library home + browse" model.
  • Critical: SEO entry pages (course details) must not feel like a "bait-and-switch".
Entry Points (Funnel Surfaces)
  • SEO (SkillUp): High traffic via specific course pages (not homepage).
  • FRS: Free articles/resources. Use contextual banners.
  • B2B: Distinct Enterprise landing page (Contact Sales).

Subscription Model

Design Principle: Simplicity. Single ladder. Highlight Pro as the hero. Keep Lite visible to maintain the "Free" promise.

Design Guardrails: Certificates

  • Name Input: "Ensure name accuracy; edits are disabled post-submission."
  • Validation: Include review step or confirmation checkbox.
  • Edge Cases: Handle profanity filters and special characters.

"Free" Positioning

  • Preserve "Free" language on SEO landing pages.
  • Trial CTAs can be styled as "Start for Free" to reduce friction.
  • Pro Trial is the priority path.

State Matrix (Logic Definitions)
  • Anonymous: Home, article, locked course preview → Plan Page → Create Account.
  • Lite (Personal): Dashboard with locked Pro cards + Lite workshops/certs.
  • Lite (Work): Lite + Bonus Advanced Workshops unlocked after work-email verification.
  • Trial (Personal): Pro access for 15 days + "days left" banner + conversion nudges.
  • Trial (Work): Pro access for 30 days + work-email verified indicator.
  • Pro (Paid): Pro unlocked; occasional Ultimate upsell for power users.
  • Ultimate (Paid): Ultimate content unlocked; no trial experience.
  • Expired: Downgraded to Lite; Pro content re-locks; Reactivate flows.
